From Building Blocks to Building Software Applications

Introduction Before i Want to Pursue Tech Field Career

Ever since i was a child, i do not know what i would be when i grow up, but eventually during my elementary years, i realized that i love building blocks from the sandbox game Minecraft and Legos in real life, i decided to become an civil engineer because my mother's cousin and her uncle is a civil engineer.

But during my junior high-school years, it was pandemic at that time, since i am chronologically with online stuff like i play games, watching online medias, social medias, and surfing websites all day during pandemic, that's when i realized, tech field could be my future career because i am very interested of creating some websites, create applications but i want to know the depth, so i decided to pursue ICT strand in my senior high-school.

My Experiences Towards The Journey of Software Developer

During my senior high-school days, my personal experiences about the ICT strand was not what i expected to be, i thought it was great because i could learn something from the professor's lectures, but in reality, i should be the to self-learn if i want to upskill, so create some pretty fun, easy, challenging, and interesting projects during my senior high-school years like i self-learn to create basic websites, coding, and other things.

The one that i made that i am proudest of was Database Management System of the school, although it was just a mock prototype, at least i learn a lot of it by self-learning, not from my professor's lectures. I remembered i almost crash out from it because i was doing it myself and AI was useless at that time, the worst of it was i made it using VB.net (which is an ancient language that almost coders does not use it or heard of it) and finding the solutions feels like i am looking for hieroglyphs from the great pyramid.

It was a struggling experience, but great at the same time because when i presented it, i feel like i was reincarnated as another person and my professor complimented me after that, that is when i realized, it's very rewarding if you self-learn and create something that you know you can learn a lot from it and gains you experience whether big or small.

My Final Decision and my Thoughts About Being a Software Developer

Now i am a 2nd year BSIT student, since i created something the things i am proud of, i decided to upskill for my future career, my final decision is to become full-stack developer and work at big tech companies like Accenture, Shopee, and other business corporate companies with great environment and decent monthly salary as well.

I am currently planning to self-learn great projects for my resume and build connections outside school to aim for landing a great job of Full-stack developer. I will also do side hustle to save money for my needs that are related to my future career.

Although i created previous projects that i am proud of, i should not be complacent, instead i should be endlessly self-learn, learn with other people that i connected with and share the mistakes and learn from other's mistakes as well especially it's a tech field, where it keeps and progressively updating, trends that never stops from the cycles, It is very important to keep learning, otherwise i would get left behind.
